<div><strong><br></strong><strong></strong><p>A photoplethysmogram (PPG) is an optically obtained <a href="http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plethysmograph">plethysmogram</a>, a volumetric measurement of an organ.</p><p>The change in volume caused by the pressure pulse is detected by illuminating the skin with the light from a LED and then measuring the amount of light either transmitted or reflected to a photodiode. Each cardiac cycle appears as a peak, as seen in the figure. Because blood flow to the skin can be modulated by multiple other physiological systems, the PPG can also be used to monitor breathing and other circulatory conditions.Additionally, the shape of the PPG waveform differs from subject to subject, and varies with the location and manner in which the pulse oximeter is attached.</p><p>The DC component of the signal is attributable to the bulk absorption of the skin tissue, while the AC component is directly attributable to variation in blood volume in the skin caused by the pressure pulse of the cardiac cycle.</p><p><img src="http://productdesign.dundee.ac.uk/~jzlawrence/blog/wp-content/uploads/2014/02/Pulse-Sensor-Single.jpg"></p><a href="https://www.sparkfun.com/products/11574">Details about the pulse sensor</a></div><h3></h3><h3><strong><br></strong></h3><h3><strong><span style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);"><br></span></strong></h3><h3><strong><span style="color: rgb(0, 0, 0);">EYE BLINK SENSOR:</span></strong></h3><p>TCRT5000</p><p><a href="http://www.vishay.com/docs/83760/tcrt5000.pdf" target="_blank">datasheet</a></p><h3><img src="http://imall.iteadstudio.com/media/catalog/product/cache/1/image/9df78eab33525d08d6e5fb8d27136e95/i/m/im120712017_2.jpg" alt="" style="float: left;
