<div style="margin-left: 20px;"><strong><span style="color: rgb(12, 12, 12);">Ultrasound sensor works as follows:</span> </strong></div>
<div style="margin-left: 20px;"><strong>The HC-SR04 Ultrasound sensor is very affordable proximity/distance sensor that has been used mainly for object avoidance in various robotics projects . It essentially gives your Arduino eyes / spacial awareness and can prevent your robot from crashing or falling off a table.
It works by sending an ultrasound pulse at around 40Khz. It then waits and listens for the pulse to echo back, calculating the time taken in microseconds (1 microsecond = 1.0 Ã— 10-6 seconds). You can trigger a pulse as fast as 20 times a second and it can determine objects up to 3 metres away and as near as 3cm. It needs a 5V power supply to run.</strong></div>

<h5><strong>3. BLUETOOTH MODULE :</strong></h5>
<div style="margin-left: 20px;"><strong>  The bluetooth module ( HC-05 ) can be used to make our robot wireless. The theory involved is that we connect bluetooth module to Arduino and send signals via serial communication from our computer bluetooth to the bluetooth module and then the arduino process it and so on.</strong></div>
<div style="margin-left: 20px;"><strong> Here is the schematic of the connection between arduino and bluetooth module HC-05:</strong></div>
<div style="margin-left: 20px;"></div><p style="margin-left: 240px;"><strong><img src="http://www.engineersgarage.com/sites/default/files/imagecache/Original/wysiwyg_imageupload/28714/LED-Blink2.png"></strong></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;"><strong><span style="color: rgb(12, 12, 12);">What we are doing here is :</span></strong></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;"><strong>a. TX pin from module is connected directly to RX pin of arduino . Bluetooth module works at 3.3V, TX pin transmit this voltage to RX pin, to which arduino can deal with.</strong></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;"><strong>c. RX pin of arduino cannot be connected directly , as arduino signal is of 5V . So, we use a voltage divider to convert it to 3.3v and then supply to TX pin of bluetooth module.</strong></p><p style="margin-left: 20px;"></p>
